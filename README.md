Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
For this task, I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador to design a one-page visual guide using Canva. My infographic focuses on three pillars: defining digital literacy, highlighting essential tools for students, and practicing safe internet habits. The goal was to make these technical concepts accessible and visually engaging for my peers. During the design process, I found it particularly interesting to balance aesthetic appeal with concise information. This exercise reinforced that being "digitally literate" isn't just about using tools, but about using them responsibly and effectively in a professional academic setting.





Task 2: Building a Professional Student Portfolio
In the modern academic and professional landscape, a digital presence is a necessity. I established my foundational presence by setting up and optimizing profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and instagram. On GitHub, I created a personalized README to introduce my academic background and learning goals. On LinkedIn, I focused on detailing my education at VIT Bhopal. These platforms will serve as a living record of my growth over the next four years, allowing me to connect with the global tech community and showcase my projects to potential mentors and employers.





Task 4: Professional Email Etiquette & Social Media
Effective communication is the backbone of professional success. I drafted two formal emails: one requesting a deadline extension from a professor and another expressing interest in an internship opportunity. Both drafts prioritize a clear subject line, a respectful tone, and a structured body. I also compiled a "Social Media Do’s and Don’ts" checklist to help students maintain a positive digital footprint. This task highlighted how a single poorly structured message can impact professional reputation, whereas clear, polite communication opens doors to academic and career opportunities
